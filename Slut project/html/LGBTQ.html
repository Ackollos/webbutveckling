What Is LGBTQ+?

LGBTQ+ is an umbrella term that includes many different sexual orientations and gender identities. Sexual orientation describes who someone is emotionally, romantically, or physically attracted to. Gender identity describes a person’s internal sense of being male, female, both, neither, or somewhere along a spectrum. These are separate parts of a person’s identity.

Different Sexual Orientations

Lesbian – A woman who is attracted to women.

Gay – A person who is attracted to the same gender (often used for men attracted to men, but can apply to others).

Bisexual – A person who is attracted to more than one gender.

Pansexual – A person who is attracted to others regardless of gender.

Asexual – A person who experiences little or no sexual attraction to others.

Queer – An umbrella term some people use to describe their sexual orientation in a broad or non-specific way.

Questioning – Someone who is still exploring or unsure about their sexual orientation.

Gender Identities

Transgender – A person whose gender identity is different from the sex they were assigned at birth.

Nonbinary – A person who does not identify strictly as male or female.

Genderfluid – A person whose gender identity may change over time.

Cisgender – A person whose gender identity matches the sex they were assigned at birth.

Understanding Identity

It is normal to question or explore your identity. Some people understand who they are early in life, while others take more time. There is no deadline for figuring it out, and it is okay if how you describe yourself changes over time.

Respect and Inclusion

Respecting others means using their chosen name and pronouns, avoiding assumptions, and treating everyone with kindness. Creating inclusive spaces helps people feel safe and accepted.
